This dissertation examines how drug-control and alternative development policies shape livelihood outcomes for smallholder coca-growing communities in Peru’s Amazon regions. The project combines satellite-derived crop data, government administrative records and spatial analysis to explore the relationship between territorial governance, coca cultivation and rural development outcomes.
