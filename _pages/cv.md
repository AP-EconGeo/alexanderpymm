## Profile

Research professional and policy analyst with experience across international development, humanitarian policy, security and governance. I combine mixed-methods research, data analysis, project delivery and policy communication across academic, humanitarian and parliamentary settings.

My technical skills include GIS, Stata, R, Excel, Google Earth Engine and Microsoft 365 workflow tools. I am experienced in translating complex evidence into clear outputs for policy, academic and humanitarian stakeholders, including ICRC teams in London and Geneva and Cabinet-level parliamentary offices.

## Key skills

**Research and analysis:** mixed-methods research design, literature reviews, expert interviews, policy analysis, survey design and analysis, econometric modelling and data visualisation.

**Data and technical:** QGIS, ArcGIS, Stata, R, advanced Excel, Google Earth Engine, satellite imagery processing, web-scraping and non-traditional dataset construction.

**Project delivery and operations:** project administration, grant administration, budget tracking, compliance monitoring, financial administration, event coordination and research data management.

**Digital and workflow tools:** Microsoft 365, SharePoint, Power Automate, Power Apps, PowerPoint, Word and basic Power BI.

**Communication and stakeholder engagement:** policy briefing, editorial coordination, newsletters and presentations to government, academic and international organisation audiences.

## Selected research and writing

### Measuring AI Harms: Adapting Frameworks to Assess AI’s Impact on Civilians in Conflict Zones

Graduate consultancy report for the ICRC UK and Ireland Delegation, produced in collaboration with LSE International Development. Developed practical approaches for identifying and assessing AI-related harms to civilians in conflict settings, drawing on evidence reviews, expert interviews and humanitarian policy analysis.

[Read sample PDF](/alexanderpymm/files/research/Pymm-ICRC-2024-Measuring-AI-Harms-Sample.pdf)

### Understanding Livelihood Outcomes in ‘Narco-Frontiers’: A Socio-Territorial Perspective

MSc dissertation, LSE, Distinction. Analysed how drug-control policies have shaped livelihood outcomes for smallholder coca growers in Peru’s Amazon regions, using satellite imagery, administrative data, QGIS and R to examine crop cultivation trends and policy effects.

[Read PDF](/alexanderpymm/files/research/Pymm-2025-Understanding-Livelihood-Outcomes-in-Narco-Frontiers.pdf)

### High Crime? The Question of Drug Revenue Incentives for Violence in Illicit Marijuana Markets

BSc dissertation, First-Class Honours. Tested theories linking illicit market revenues and organised criminal violence using web-scraped price data from US-Mexico drug markets. Dataset construction and instrumental variable estimation in Stata.

[Read PDF](/alexanderpymm/files/research/Pymm-2021-High-Crime-Drug-Revenue-Incentives-for-Violence.pdf)
