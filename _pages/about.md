I am an applied researcher working across illicit economies, security and development policy. My work combines political economy, spatial analysis and policy research to examine how complex systems of governance, violence and livelihoods shape development outcomes.

I am currently based at University College London’s Institute for Global Prosperity, where I support research delivery, project coordination and data-management workflows across multidisciplinary academic projects. My wider experience spans humanitarian consultancy for the International Committee of the Red Cross, research and communications work in UK Parliament, and academic research on illicit markets, conflict and rural development.

## Research interests

My current research interests include:

- illicit economies, organised crime and drug policy;
- security, conflict and civilian harm;
- rural development and livelihood transitions;
- territorial governance and political economy;
- GIS, spatial data analysis and mixed-methods research design.

A recurring focus of my work is how policy interventions affect communities situated at the margins of formal and illicit economies. My recent research has examined coca cultivation and alternative development policy in the Peruvian Amazon, using satellite imagery, administrative data and spatial analysis to understand how drug-control policies shape livelihood outcomes for smallholder farmers.

## Applied research and policy work

I have worked across research, policy and project-delivery settings, including UCL’s Institute for Global Prosperity, the International Committee of the Red Cross, and the UK Parliament. Across these roles, I have produced policy briefings, research reports, stakeholder-facing outputs, data workflows and public communications for academic, humanitarian and government audiences.

My technical work includes GIS and spatial analysis, dataset construction, econometric modelling, qualitative evidence review, expert interviews, Excel/Power Query workflows and research-data management. I am particularly interested in research that connects rigorous empirical analysis with practical decision-making in security, development and humanitarian contexts.

## Selected research

- **Measuring AI Harms: Adapting Frameworks to Assess AI’s Impact on Civilians in Conflict Zones** — graduate consultancy research for the ICRC’s UK and Geneva offices.
- **Understanding Livelihood Outcomes in ‘Narco-Frontiers’: A Socio-Territorial Perspective** — MSc dissertation, LSE, on coca cultivation, alternative development and territorial governance in Peru.
- **High Crime? The Question of Drug Revenue Incentives for Violence in Illicit Marijuana Markets** — undergraduate dissertation, King’s College London, using web-scraped market data to examine organised criminal violence.
